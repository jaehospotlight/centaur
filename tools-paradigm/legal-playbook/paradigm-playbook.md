# Paradigm Legal Playbook

## Core Operating Principles

1. Economics are paramount: valuation, investment amount, option pool, board seat/control dynamics.
2. Move fast without burning founder trust; preserve relationship quality while negotiating.
3. Most non-economic term-sheet edits are negotiable; calibrate asks to deal context and speed pressure.
4. Do not run "gotcha" diligence. Surface risks early so the company can fix them before later financing/M&A.
5. Stage matters: governance asks that are standard when leading early may be unrealistic in late-stage minority checks.

## Financing Lifecycle (Default Sequence)

1. Draft term sheet from deal-closing thread economics.
2. Request minimally missing info from DRI, then circulate a fully populated draft.
3. Negotiate comments with a speed/relationship lens; escalate economics to investment team.
4. After term sheet is set: kick off counsel process and diligence.
5. Review main docs in order: Charter, SPA, IRA, Voting, ROFR (plus other primary docs as needed).
6. Review ancillary docs (board consent, stockholder consent, officer/secretary certificates, MRL, opinion, side docs).
7. Move to substantially final docs, collect signatures into escrow, then close/fund.

## Negotiation Priority Ladder

1. Economics: valuation, check size, ownership math, option pool, board/control.
2. Critical legal protections: anti-dilution, liquidation structure, amendment controls, sanctions/OISP coverage.
3. Structure/cleanup terms and drafting nits.
4. Cosmetic language and low-impact style edits.

## Stage-Calibrated Governance and Control

- Strong ask for Paradigm protective-consent rights when Paradigm is an early-stage lead investor.
- If Paradigm is late-stage and a small holder, lack of a bespoke block may be acceptable with explicit rationale.
- Always explain if a governance concession is driven by stage, ownership, or speed/competitive pressure.
- Token consent:
  - If token path is plausible, include explicit Paradigm consent protections.
  - If tokens are unlikely, keep token block unless there is a reason to trade it away for speed/relationship.

## Diligence Priorities

### Cap Table Integrity

- Tie out each issuance to both:
  - authorizing board/stockholder approval, and
  - signed grant/issuance documentation.
- Flag ambiguities immediately (promised grants, missing approvals, mismatched schedules).

### IP Integrity

- Confirm company ownership chain for core IP.
- Check founder/employee/consultant assignment coverage.
- Review open-source strategy and material agreements for harmful IP assignment leakage.

### Regulatory Process Checks

- Rule 506 / Bad Actor readiness.
- Blue sky filing plan and state coverage.
- HSR and CFIUS triggers where applicable.
- OISP screening where China-linked and covered-sector facts are present.

## Main Document Focus

- Charter: NVCA baseline deviations, economics/control terms, protective provisions tie-out to term sheet.
- SPA: reps/warranties (including QSBS, FCPA/sanctions/OISP where relevant), purchaser schedule accuracy.
- IRA: competitor carve-out, Major Investor mechanics, info rights, Section 220 posture, ROFO math, covenant package.
- Voting: board designation mechanics, drag/service requirements, amendment controls.
- ROFR/Co-sale: transfer carve-outs, amendment/service constraints, pledge and exception thresholds.

## Closing Mechanics

- Circulate substantially final docs and gather signatures in escrow.
- Lead investor typically funds first; followers may wait for lead wire confirmation.
- Confirm charter filing and signature completeness before final funding instruction.
- Confirm company receipt of funds and complete post-closing filings/cleanup.

## Red Lines (Non-Negotiable or Conditionally Non-Negotiable)

1. Anti-dilution is broad-based weighted average (never full ratchet).
2. Liquidation preference is 1x non-participating.
3. Paradigm blocking rights when Paradigm is leading with meaningful ownership.
4. Token issuance requires Paradigm consent (except warrant carve-out).
5. IP/token transfer treated as deemed liquidation event.
6. SPA includes sanctions and outbound investment reps where required.
7. MRL names Paradigm Fund LP and Paradigm Two LP where applicable.
8. Major Investor qualification and rights are explicit (or variance is surfaced).
9. Competitor carve-out excludes Paradigm and affiliates in all relevant agreements.
10. DGCL Section 220 rights are not waived.
11. Paradigm-specific provisions require Paradigm written consent to amend.
12. Rights parity: flag rights granted to others but not Paradigm.
13. Sanctions provisions appear consistently throughout the deal package.
14. Token warrants default to net exercise.
15. Lockup terms are not more onerous than insider lockups.
16. Smart contract restrictions require Paradigm consent where applicable.

## Nice To Haves

- Founder carveout target 2% (5% acceptable).
- ROFR window target 30 days (45 acceptable).
- Competitor list controls.
- Generative AI representation language.

## Standard Term Sheet Defaults

- Debt threshold: $1M
- Qualified IPO threshold: >$100M net proceeds
- Option pool: 10-15%
- Token floor: 50% for crypto deals
- Legal fee cap: $75,000
- No-shop period: 45 days
- NVCA reference: 2025 forms

## Severity Calibration

- `RED_LINE`: explicit playbook red-line breach with sufficient evidence.
- `STANDARD`: negotiable market/playbook deviation or conditional item not triggered.
- `NICE_TO_HAVE`: optimization; include in redlines but lower negotiation priority.
- If applicability is conditional (lead status, ownership size, token relevance), state condition and severity rationale.
- If source evidence is incomplete, use `STANDARD` plus targeted follow-up questions.

## Drafting Language Preferences

- Anti-dilution: broad-based weighted-average framing; never full-ratchet language.
- Liquidation preference: 1x non-participating default, especially for Paradigm-led rounds.
- Amendment vetoes: require Company + Requisite Holders + affected-investor consent for adverse differential amendments.
- Sanctions/OISP: include sanctions language and outbound investment language (31 C.F.R. Part 850) across SPA and related docs.
- Favor concise, practical drafting and preserve negotiation flexibility where issues are not economic or red-line critical.
